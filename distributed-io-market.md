<p><p>Distributed I/O markets can be categorized into analog and digital types based on signal processing. Analog I/O markets handle continuous signals, allowing for real-time monitoring and control in environments like industrial automation. They offer granular data granularity and are essential for tasks requiring precise measurements. In contrast, digital I/O markets process discrete signals, enabling binary data operations such as on/off switching and status monitoring. These systems are commonly used in digital communication, automation, and control applications, ensuring clear signal integrity and efficiency.</p></p>
