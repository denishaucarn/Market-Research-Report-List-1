<p><ul><li>Direct Thrombin Inhibitor</li><li>Factor Xa Inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Novel Oral Anticoagulant (NOAC) drugs market is primarily segmented into two main types: Direct Thrombin Inhibitors and Factor Xa Inhibitors. Direct Thrombin Inhibitors, such as dabigatran, target thrombin directly to prevent clot formation. Factor Xa Inhibitors, including rivaroxaban and apixaban, inhibit Factor Xa, an essential component in the coagulation cascade, effectively reducing thrombus development. Both classes offer advantages over traditional anticoagulants, such as fixed dosing and reduced monitoring requirements, enhancing patient adherence and safety.</p></p>
